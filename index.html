About Hammock Earth

Hammock Earth is a community dedicated to cultivating ecological belonging through shared experiences with land, food, and one another.

We believe that human well-being and ecological health are inseparable. When we reconnect with the land that nourishes us—through food, seasons, soil, and community—we rediscover a deeper sense of belonging and responsibility for the living world.

Our work creates spaces where people can slow down, reconnect with their senses, and experience the culture that grows food: the relationships between farmers and land, the care behind regenerative practices, and the communities that gather around the table.

Through farm tours, communal meals, workshops, storytelling, and immersive gatherings, Hammock Earth invites people into a living relationship with the ecosystems that sustain us.

Our hope is to nurture a joyful and generous Canadian food culture that not only tastes delicious but also regenerates soil, strengthens communities, and supports climate resilience for generations to come.

Our Vision

To grow global well-being through ecological belonging.

Ecological belonging is remembering that we are part of nature, not separate from it. Our well-being is shaped by how we care for the land that sustains us—our food systems, animals, water, and one another across generations.

It is the lived experience of being in relationship with land, food, seasons, and community, where care for the earth and care for people are understood as the same act.

What We're Here to Do

Our Story

How Hammock Earth Began

How do we care for the Heart and the Earth at the same time?

Hammock Earth began as a shared inquiry between two experienced entrepreneurs asking the same question: How do we care for the Heart and the Earth at the same time?

Thu Nguyen and Anahita first met through a Coralus community pod and later gathered in person at Nusqool. As their friendship deepened—through walks, meals, a spa day, and a temple sound bath—they realized their work had been evolving in parallel.

Thu had been building a global community of helpers, healers, facilitators, and educators through her coaching and healing work. At the same time, Anahita had been stewarding land as an organic grower, experimenting with regenerative approaches to farming and exploring new ways to cultivate healthier food systems.

Together they recognized a shared calling: to create fertile ground—both in soil and in community—where people can reconnect with land, actualize their gifts, and experience ecological belonging.

Hammock Earth grew from that vision. Today, it continues to bring people together through food, land, and community to nurture a culture of care for both people and planet.

Our Co-Founders

The People Behind the Land

Thu Nguyen

Somatic Leadership Coach & Community Builder

Thu Nguyen is an entrepreneur, somatic leadership coach, and community builder dedicated to cultivating spaces where people can reconnect with themselves, one another, and the living earth.

After two decades working in technology and startups, a series of life-altering health experiences led Thu to step away from the tech world and explore a deeper path of healing, embodiment, and nature connection. Along the way, she brought together a global community of helpers, healers, facilitators, and educators through retreats, circles, and shared learning experiences.

Through Nusqool, Thu discovered the power of bringing people into relationship with land, food, and one another. Today, with Hammock Earth, Thu focuses on creating experiences that cultivate ecological belonging: inviting people to slow down, reconnect with the natural world, and rediscover the joy of being part of a living ecosystem.

Anahita Belanger

Regenerative Leader & Organic Grower

Anahita Belanger is a regenerative leader and organic grower rooted in ecological connection and conscious food systems. She pasture-raises heritage hogs and hens and grows vegetables and medicinal plants on her certified organic farm, blending her reverence for nature with a commitment to nourish both people and planet.

Anahita's work bridges local food, farm experiences, and community engagement. She has curated food experiences that bring eaters into direct relationship with local farms and has supported small food and farm entrepreneurs as an advisor in mission-aligned finance. She is also a founding activator in a global network supporting women-led ventures.

Anahita is devoted to regenerating land, strengthening local food systems, and building community. Combining her experience in ecological agriculture, hospitality, and start-up operations, she creates hands-on land stewardship workshops, immersive local food experiences, and collaborative gatherings where people reconnect with nature and rediscover ecological belonging—remembering that human well-being and the planet's vitality are inseparable.
